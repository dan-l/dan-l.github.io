GoTron

 2016

Peer-to-peer Tron game with a centralized matchmaking server

This peer-to-peer game is an alternate design to conventional games that uses an exclusive central server. It allows for a larger bandwidth by sharing the load with its peers and elimnates the single-point of failure in the single server model while maintaning game state consistency.

Players are able to control their characters in the tron game while also see their peer's movement updated near real time on their respective screens. The usual rules of a tron game applies and players will see the same winner across all screens.

Wutudu

 2015

Mobile web app that brings people together by intelligently organizing social gatherings.

This app enables a group of friends hanging out to decide on “what to do”. It will ask every participant 10 questions, which is use to decide on a single activity for the group. The app considers factors such as user's location, time and preference constraints. Once an activity is decided, all participants will get a real time native notification about it.

Deciding and agreeing on a particular activity is often a strenous task for groups. This app aims to solve that problem by providing entertainment to group of friends with minimal effort required of them. After participants answered those questions, the app will then compile a list of potential locations and rank them based on factors mentioned above to generate the choice of entertainment for the group.

Vibernate

 2015

Android app that conveniently schedules user's phone to switch to a specified ringtone profile at a recurring specific time

This material designed app provides students, who often have busy class schedules, a slick user interface to automate their phone ringtone profile. Users will be able to have different ringtone profile with varying duration set according to their own planned schedule.

Users can schedule all of their profiles ahead of time once and easily edit all the information later. The app displays the ringtone profiles in a visually attractive way through the schedule view and the list view. Ringtone profiles can be temporarily snoozed and turn back on later. Users can also personalize ringtone profiles with different color themes.

WheresTheFood

 2014

Web app where users can explore and share with others their favorite local food vendors in Vancouver

This food vendor web app shows a list of food vendors in Vancouver that is parsed from a dataset. Users can find each of the food vendors plotted on Google Maps. There are options for users to quickly search for a particular food vendor, or filter the visible food vendors on map by favorite, ratings or those that users have left a memo about.

Clicking on each of the food vendor markers on the map which display additional information about each food vendor such as description and address. There is also a profile page for each of the food vendor where users are able to write up private memos about the food vendor, favorite it, rate it, comment on it and view relevant instagram photos about the food vendor. Users can only remove their own comments.
